Smothered Pork Chops

An All-Time Favorite Low country Dish

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in Pork Chops (1-inch thick)
  • 1 Large Onion, sliced thin
  • 2 Cloves of Garlic, minced
  • 1/2 Cup Of All-purpose flour
  • 2 cups of Chicken Broth
  • 1/2 cup of your favorite cooking oil
  • 1/2 tablespoon of your favorite seasoning salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon each: garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter (optional)

    • Instructions

    1. In a shallow bowl, mix together the flour and seasonings.
    2. Cover the pork chops with the flour mixture, pressing it onto the meat to ensure it sticks.
    3. Make sure to coat both sides evenly. Set aside.
    4. In a large skillet, heat the cooking oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the pork chops and cook for about 3 minutes on each side until they are golden brown. Remove the pork chops from the skillet and set them aside on a plate.
    5. The pork chops will not be fully cooked at this point, but they will finish cooking in the gravy later.
    6. In the same skillet, add the sliced onions and cook for about 5 minutes until they are soft and translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.
    7. Sprinkle the remaining flour mixture over the onions and garlic, stirring well to combine. Cook for about 1-2 minutes to cook off the raw flour taste.
    8. Gradually pour in the chicken broth while stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Continue to cook and stir until the gravy thickens, about 3-5 minutes.
    9. Add the tablespoon of unsalted butter to the gravy and stir until melted and well combined. This will add richness to the gravy, but it is optional.
    10. Return the pork chops to the skillet, nestling them into the gravy. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the pork chops are cooked through and tender.
    11. Once the pork chops are cooked, give the gravy a final stir and adjust the seasoning if needed. Serve the smothered pork chops hot, spooning the delicious gravy over the top. Enjoy!

    Serving Suggestion: Serve the smothered pork chops with mashed potatoes, rice or even egg noodles. But I highly recommend the lowcountry way with rice!
